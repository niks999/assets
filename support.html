Installation & Setup

How to Install

  1. Visit the Google Workspace Marketplace
  2. Search for "Calendar Conflict Auto-Decliner"
  3. Click "Install" and grant the necessary permissions
  4. Open Google Calendar and look for the add-on in your sidebar
  5. Configure your settings in the add-on panel

Required Permissions

The add-on requires these permissions to function:

  • calendar.events - Read and modify calendar events for conflict detection
  • userinfo.email - Identify your email address for proper event handling
  • calendar.addons.execute - Function as a Google Calendar add-on
  • script.scriptapp - Manage automated triggers for scheduled checking

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How does the add-on detect conflicts?

A: The add-on looks for overlapping time slots between your accepted meetings and pending invitations. It considers meetings conflicting if they overlap by more than the minimum duration you've configured (default: 15 minutes).

Q: What are protected keywords?

A: Protected keywords are terms that, when found in a meeting title or description, prevent the meeting from being automatically declined. Default keywords include "1:1", "one-on-one", "performance review", "urgent", and "important".

Q: Can I customize which meetings are protected?

A: Yes! You can:

  • Add or remove protected keywords
  • Enable protection for meetings you've created
  • Adjust the lookback and lookahead time windows
  • Set minimum meeting duration thresholds

Q: What is "Dry Run" mode?

A: Dry Run mode shows you which meetings would be declined without actually declining them. This helps you test your configuration before enabling automatic processing.

Q: How often does the add-on check for conflicts?

A: You can configure automatic checking to run every 15 minutes, hourly, or daily. You can also run manual checks anytime from the add-on interface.

Q: Will the add-on decline important meetings?

A: The add-on has multiple safeguards:

  • Protected keywords prevent declining important meetings
  • You can protect meetings you've created
  • Dry run mode lets you preview actions
  • You can always manually accept meetings after they're declined

Troubleshooting

Add-on Not Appearing in Google Calendar

  1. Refresh your Google Calendar tab
  2. Check that you installed from the correct Google account
  3. Verify the add-on appears in your Google Workspace Admin Console (if applicable)
  4. Try logging out and back into Google Calendar

Conflicts Not Being Detected

  1. Check your lookback/lookahead day settings
  2. Verify the minimum meeting duration setting
  3. Ensure you have pending invitations (not just accepted meetings)
  4. Run a manual check to see if conflicts are detected

Settings Not Saving

  1. Make sure you click "Save Settings" after making changes
  2. Check that you have proper permissions granted
  3. Try refreshing the add-on panel
  4. Verify your Google account has sufficient storage

Automatic Processing Not Working

  1. Ensure "Enable Auto Processing" is turned on
  2. Check that you've set up a trigger schedule
  3. Verify the trigger appears in your Google Apps Script dashboard
  4. Make sure your Google account permissions haven't changed
